More freedom, not less

A list of laws and customs that are fucked up and need to be fixed.

Curated by Soham Sankaran.

Preamble

I have a simple political philosophy: more freedom, not less. In particular, this means more freedom for the little guy, for the independent thinker, the protestor, the iconoclast, the conscientious objecter -- dissent must not be grounds for persecution, much less prosecution, and difference must not lead to discrimination.

I built this page after observing a repeated pattern of behaviour: I read or hear about some law or custom that obviously runs counter to those ideals, get worked up about it for a few days, then forget the exact details after I find the next fucked up thing. At the end of many, many iterations of this cycle, I'm left only with a lot of unresolved residual anger and have done very little to fix the underlying issues.

This list is an attempt to catalogue each of these fucked up things as I find them, as well as to track efforts to fix them.

If you have something to add to this list, email me at soham [at] soh (dot) am.

The Issues

Issue #01: Civil Forfeiture

aka civil asset foreiture, civil seizure, civil judicial forfeiture.

What the fuck is this?

Civil forfeiture laws allow police to sieze and sell any property they claim is involved in a crime. No criminal charge or conviction of the owner of said property is required.

Where is this a problem?

The United States - in particular, Alabama, Alaska, Delaware, Illinois, Massachusetts, Missouri, Rhode Island, South Carolina, Wyoming, Georgia, South Dakota and Washington.

Who gets this right?

New Mexico and Nebraska - civil forefeiture is abolshished under state law... except it seems like police might just be ignoring those laws, at least in New Mexico.

Why is this evil?

Put simply, this is legalized theft - the police accuse you of intending to commit a crime, take your money, then put that money into the a police department slush fund. A number of police departments have been found to openly have a quota system in place to encourage and even require these seizures. A lot of those departments rely on such seizures as their main sources of funding.
